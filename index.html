Getting Started

  1. Grant photo access. When you first open SwipeSort, grant Full Access to your photo library so all your media can be sorted.
  2. Create albums. Head to the Albums tab and tap the + button to create custom albums before you start sorting.
  3. Start swiping. Return to the Photos tab and swipe through your library. Swipe right to organize, left to delete, or up to skip.
  4. Switch to Videos. Tap the Videos toggle at the top of the Photos tab to sort your video library the same way.
  5. Review albums. Open any album to view, move, or delete photos and videos in bulk.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the daily swipe limit?

Free users can perform 100 swipe actions per day (organize, delete, or skip). The limit resets every day at midnight. You can earn up to +10 bonus swipes per rewarded ad, with a maximum of 5 rewarded ads per day. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited swipes.

How do I earn extra swipes?

On the Albums tab, tap the “Earn Swipes” card when you’ve reached your daily limit. Watch a short rewarded video ad to receive +10 swipes. You can do this up to 5 times per day (up to +50 bonus swipes).

Does SwipeSort work offline?

Yes. All photo and video processing happens entirely on your device. No internet connection is needed for sorting, organizing, or deleting. An internet connection is only required to load ads (free tier) or to restore your Pro subscription.

Are my photos private?

Absolutely. SwipeSort never uploads, transmits, or stores your photos or videos on any external server. All organization happens locally on your device. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Can I sort videos as well as photos?

Yes. Tap the Videos toggle at the top of the Photos tab to switch to video-sorting mode. The same swipe gestures apply — right to organize, left to delete, up to skip. Tap the play button on a video card to watch it before deciding.

Can I undo my actions?

Yes. After any swipe action, an Undo button appears. Tap it immediately to reverse. Additionally, deleted photos and videos are moved to “Recently Deleted” in your iOS photo library where they can be recovered for up to 30 days.

How many albums can I create?

Free users can create up to 5 custom albums. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited albums.

What happens when I delete a photo?

Deleted photos and videos are moved to the iOS “Recently Deleted” album, where they stay for up to 30 days before being permanently removed. You can restore them from there at any time via the Photos app.

Can I move photos between albums?

Yes. Open any album, then long-press a photo to enter multi-select mode. Select the photos you want to move and tap the Move button to transfer them to another album.

Does SwipeSort modify my original photos?

No. SwipeSort only organizes photos into albums and flags items for deletion. Original photo data, metadata, and EXIF information are never modified.

Will my albums sync across my devices?

Albums created in SwipeSort are stored in your iOS photo library. If you have iCloud Photos enabled, your albums will sync across your devices automatically via Apple’s iCloud — this syncing is controlled by Apple, not SwipeSort.

How do I cancel my Pro subscription?

Go to iOS Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions → SwipeSort and tap Cancel Subscription. You will retain Pro access until the end of the current billing period. For refunds, visit reportaproblem.apple.com.

How do I restore my Pro subscription after reinstalling?

Open SwipeSort, go to Settings → Restore Purchases and tap Restore. Your subscription will be verified with Apple and Pro features will be re-enabled automatically.

What are Sort Reminders?

If you enable Sort Reminders in Settings, SwipeSort will send a local notification on Saturday evenings reminding you to sort your photos. No data leaves your device — reminders are scheduled entirely on-device by iOS.

How do I zoom in on a photo?

Double-tap any photo while swiping to open the full-screen viewer. From there you can pinch to zoom, pan around, and examine the photo in detail before deciding what to do with it.

What are Sort Stats? Pro

Sort Stats is a Pro-exclusive screen that tracks your sorting activity: swipes today, this week, all-time total, and your current daily streak. Access it from the Settings tab.

Troubleshooting

App is not showing my photos or videos

Make sure SwipeSort has Full Access to your photo library:

  1. Open the iOS Settings app.
  2. Scroll down and tap SwipeSort.
  3. Tap Photos.
  4. Select Full Access.

Photos or videos are loading slowly

If iCloud Photos is enabled, some media may be stored in the cloud and needs to download first. Ensure you have a stable internet connection. You can also open the iOS Photos app and let it sync before using SwipeSort.

Swipe limit reached unexpectedly

The daily limit of 100 swipes resets at midnight local time each day. You can earn up to +50 bonus swipes per day by watching rewarded video ads — tap the Earn Swipes card on the Albums tab. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited swipes.

Pro features not showing after purchase

Go to Settings → Restore Purchases in the app. If the issue persists, ensure you’re signed in to the same Apple ID used for the purchase, then try again. If you still have trouble, contact us below.

Undo button not appearing

The undo button appears immediately after a swipe action and disappears once you perform another action. Act quickly if you need to reverse a swipe.

Can’t delete an album

System albums like Favorites and Recently Deleted cannot be deleted. All custom albums you created can be removed by tapping the trash icon next to them in the Albums tab.

Video playback issues

If a video doesn’t play in SwipeSort, check that the video file has finished downloading from iCloud. In the iOS Photos app, tap the video and wait for the download to complete, then return to SwipeSort.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Create albums before sorting — set up your destination albums first so you can organize in one fluid session without interruption.
  • Use descriptive album names — clear names like “Vacation 2025” or “Screenshots” make it easier to find photos later.
  • Sort in themed batches — focus on one category at a time for faster, more consistent decisions.
  • Swipe up to skip, not delete — if you’re unsure about a photo, swipe up to skip it. Come back to skipped photos in a later session.
  • Double-tap to zoom — check photo quality before deleting by double-tapping to open the full-screen viewer.
  • Earn bonus swipes daily — watch up to 5 rewarded ads per day for +10 swipes each if you’re on the free tier.
  • Review Recently Deleted — periodically check the iOS Recently Deleted album before photos are permanently removed after 30 days.
  • Use bulk select for fast cleanup — long-press in albums to select multiple items for moving or deleting at once.
  • Pull to refresh — pull down on the Albums tab to refresh your photo library after making changes externally.
